I am a PhD Researcher specializing in the intersection of **Explainable AI (XAI)** and **Control Systems** for Connected and Automated Vehicles (CAVs). My work focuses on bridging the gap between high-performance, "black-box" driving models and the safety-critical interpretability required for real-world deployment.

Currently, I research how to make autonomous systems both technically robust and human-centric. I also lead the CAV team for **EcoCAR**, where I manage the hardware-in-the-loop integration of V2X, Autonomous Intelligent Nodes (AIN), and Adaptive Cruise Control (ACC) systems into physical vehicles.

### Education
* **Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering**, *The University of Texas at Austin* (Current)
* **M.S. in Mechanical Engineering**, *The University of Texas at Austin* (2024)
* **B.S. in Mechanical Engineering**, *Yonsei University* (2023)
  
### Research Focus
My recent work addresses the physical and computational challenges of high-speed autonomy:
* **Explainable AI:** Using Explainable Boosting Machines (EBMs) to create data-efficient, interpretable approximations of black-box driving models.
* **Control Theory:** Designing high-speed path-tracking control laws that actively compensate for time-delayed steering system dynamics in physical vehicles.
* **Human-Machine Interfaces (HMI):** Evaluating the human impact of visual detail in vehicle lane-keeping system communication.

### Technical Arsenal
* **Software:** C++, Python (PyTorch, Scikit-Learn)
* **Hardware & Systems:** ROS/ROS2, MATLAB/Simulink, V2X Integration
* **Specialties:** Sensor Integration, Path Planning, Time-Delay Systems, System Architecture
