The EcoCAR Mobility Challenge is a premier engineering competition where universities are tasked with engineering a next-generation battery electric vehicle. As the **Connected and Automated Vehicle (CAV) Team Lead** for The University of Texas at Austin, I manage the architecture, testing, and deployment of advanced autonomous features on a physical production vehicle.

Moving from theoretical simulation to physical hardware introduces massive complexities. My role bridges the gap between software development and vehicle-level integration.

### Core Responsibilities & Technical Milestones

* **Team Leadership & System Architecture:** Direct a cross-functional team of engineers using agile methodologies to design, test, and validate software-in-the-loop (SIL) and hardware-in-the-loop (HIL) systems.
* **V2X Communication Integration:** Architected the Vehicle-to-Everything (V2X) pipeline, allowing the vehicle to ingest and react to real-time traffic signal data and infrastructure broadcasts to optimize speed profiles and energy efficiency.
* **Autonomous Intelligent Nodes (AIN):** Led the deployment of edge-computing nodes to handle high-bandwidth sensor data processing (camera, radar, LiDAR) directly on the vehicle network.
* **Adaptive Cruise Control (ACC) Deployment:** Guided the transition of longitudinal control algorithms from MATLAB/Simulink into C++ ROS nodes, successfully executing closed-loop vehicle control during physical track testing.

**Impact:** This project proves my ability to take complex control theories out of the lab and safely deploy them onto physical vehicles under strict deadlines and rigorous safety standards.
